My Path to Consulting

Where I'm Headed

After finishing up my Finance degree at BYU, my next step is joining Cicero Group as a Business Analyst Intern. My goal is to work in management consulting, where I can help companies figure out where they are losing money or missing opportunities, and build frameworks to fix those issues.

How I Got Here

I didn't start out in a traditional finance track. I actually spent years running a landscaping startup in the Salt Lake City area, which taught me the basics of route optimization, hiring, and payroll. From there, I wanted to understand businesses at a higher level, which led me to internships in real estate underwriting at Extra Space Storage and private equity at Solen Software Group. Winning the Bain Case Competition recently was a big validation that I enjoy the fast-paced, problem-solving environment of consulting.

What I Want to Contribute

I think a lot of standard financial modeling is going to be automated in the next few years. Because of my experience deploying AI-powered valuation models, I want to be the person who bridges the gap between old-school DCF/LBO analysis and new AI automation tools, helping clients scale faster without losing accuracy.
